WHY SPLIT 90/110? (THE MOST IMPORTANT STRUCTURAL DECISION)

============================================================================

----------------------------------------------------------------------

THE FUNDAMENTAL PROBLEM WE'RE SOLVING

----------------------------------------------------------------------

Insurance has TWO very different types of risk:

LONG-TERM RISK:
- Death, critical illness, disability
- Claims happen RARELY (once in a lifetime)
- Claims are SEVERE (hundreds of thousands to millions)
- Pricing is based on MORTALITY TABLES (30-year horizons)
- Customers expect STABILITY ("My family is always protected")

SHORT-TERM RISK:
- Travel incidents, sports injuries, purchase issues
- Claims happen FREQUENTLY (multiple times per year)
- Claims are MODERATE (thousands to tens of thousands)
- Pricing can be based on REAL-TIME DATA
- Customers expect FLEXIBILITY ("Cover me when I need it")

----------------------------------------------------------------------

YOU CANNOT TREAT THEM THE SAME

----------------------------------------------------------------------

If you try to make life insurance "dynamic":
- Regulators won't approve it (life requires stable, predictable policies)
- Customers won't buy it ("My death benefit shouldn't change because I went hiking")
- Actuaries can't price it (no real-time data for death risk)

If you try to make general insurance "fixed":
- Customers overpay (paying for travel coverage when not traveling)
- Customers underprotected (not enough activity coverage when doing sports)
- Profitability suffers (insurer exposed to wrong risks)

----------------------------------------------------------------------

THE SOLUTION: SEPARATE THEM

----------------------------------------------------------------------

HKD 90 = FIXED LIFE INSURANCE

This buys:
- Term life: ~HKD 1,000,000 death benefit
- Critical illness: ~HKD 200,000 lump sum
- Disability income: ~HKD 10,000/month for 24 months

Why HKD 90?
- Based on HSBC Life's actual pricing for term life
- Competitive with market rates (HKD 80-120/month for similar coverage)
- Sufficient to fund HKD 1M sum assured for 30-year-old

This NEVER CHANGES because:
- Regulatory requirement (life policies must be stable)
- Customer expectation (family protection shouldn't fluctuate)
- Actuarial reality (death risk is long-term, not daily)

----------------------------------------------------------------------

HKD 110 = DYNAMIC GENERAL INSURANCE

This buys real-time coverage for:
- Travel (medical, disruption, baggage)
- Activity (sports injury, accident)
- Purchase (fraud, damage, non-delivery)
- Property (home contents, personal belongings)
- Cyber (identity theft, online fraud)
- Liability (third-party injury/damage)

Why HKD 110?
- Remaining budget after life (HKD 200 - HKD 90 = HKD 110)
- Sufficient to fund meaningful real-time coverage
- Pools risk across all general categories

This CONSTANTLY CHANGES because:
- Real-time data enables it (card auth in 1-3 seconds)
- Customers expect it (cover me for what I'm doing NOW)
- Profitability requires it (match coverage to actual risk)

----------------------------------------------------------------------
THE PAYOUT FORMULA
----------------------------------------------------------------------

For Non-Parametric Categories (Health, Travel, Liability, Cyber, Property):

    Actual Payout = MIN(Actual Financial Loss, Allocated Coverage × Multiplier)

For Parametric Categories (weather-triggered events):

    Actual Payout = Allocated Coverage × Multiplier × Trigger Percentage

Example: If you have $20 allocated to Travel and file a $480 claim:
- Maximum coverage = $20 × 150 = $3,000
- Actual loss = $480
- Payout = MIN($480, $3,000) = $480

PART 4: LIQUIDITY BUFFER MANAGEMENT
============================================================================

----------------------------------------------------------------------
WHY WE NEED LIQUIDITY TIERS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

To support sub-second payouts (especially parametric triggers like typhoons
and flight delays), the $110 short-term pool is maintained in tiered structure:

Tier 1 (Instant):  $22 (20%) - Cash/overnight deposits - Seconds settlement
Tier 2 (Same-day): $55 (50%) - T-bills/money market funds - Hours settlement
Tier 3 (Reserve):  $33 (30%) - Ultra-short bonds - 1-2 days settlement

This ensures:
- Tier 1 can handle 10+ concurrent $2,200 parametric claims
- No fire sale of long-term assets needed
- T-bills and MMF count as HQLA under HKIA rules
- Customer claims paid instantly

----------------------------------------------------------------------
REPLENISHMENT RULES
----------------------------------------------------------------------

- If Tier 1 falls below $10: Next premium replenishes to $22 first
- If Tier 2 falls below $30: Auto-transfer from Tier 3 (executed at 2AM)
- Catastrophe override: HSBC group liquidity facility (48-hour access)

MECHANISM 1: REAL-TIME RISK MATCHING

You pay for coverage you don't use:

Example:

- Customer overpaid for unused coverage

You don't get coverage when you need it:

Example:

- Customer travels to Thailand
- Home insurance is active, but customer isn't home
- Travel insurance is IN
