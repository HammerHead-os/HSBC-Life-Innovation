1. WHY THIS PRODUCT MAKES MONEY — THE SHORT VERSION

The Micro-Protection Fluid does not ask customers to pay more. It makes HSBC
keep more of what customers already pay. The profit comes from five sources:
structurally lower claims costs, zero customer acquisition cost, reduced churn,
cheaper reinsurance, and a future platform fee model. Each is backed by

out less in claims per dollar of premium collected.

The Micro-Protection Fluid advantage over telematics:
Telematics only covers driving. The Fluid covers the customer's entire risk
profile across all general insurance categories. The reallocation is not just
pricing — it is exposure management. When a customer is not traveling, HSBC
is not exposed to travel claims. When they are not doing sports, HSBC is not
exposed to sports injury claims. The insurer's actual exposure at any moment
matches what it is being compensated for.

Conservative estimate:
- Traditional general insurance loss ratio: 65-70%
- Micro-Protection Fluid target loss ratio: 40-50%
- Improvement: 15-25 percentage points

This is conservative because:
(a) The product only covers general insurance (travel, purchase, activity),
    which has lower severity than health or auto
(b) Real-time reallocation means near-zero "silent exposure" — the industry
    term for risk the insurer carries but is not adequately pricing for
(c) The floor-and-ceiling model caps maximum exposure per category

Revenue impact at scale:
- 5 million customers × $100/month average premium = $6B annual premium
- 20 percentage point loss ratio improvement = $1.2B additional retained
  revenue per year
- Even at 2 million customers and 15 points improvement = $360M/year

5. PROFIT LEVER 4 — REINSURANCE SAVINGS

Traditional reinsurance problem:
Insurers buy reinsurance based on estimated maximum exposure across their
entire book. Because traditional policies have fixed allocations, the insurer
must assume worst-case exposure in every category simultaneously. This means
buying more reinsurance than is statistically necessary.

The Fluid advantage:
HSBC knows, in real time, exactly how much exposure it carries in each
category across its entire customer base. On any given Tuesday at 3pm, HSBC
can tell its reinsurer: "We have $X in travel exposure, $Y in purchase
protection exposure, $Z in activity exposure." This granularity allows HSBC
to buy precise, dynamic reinsurance rather than broad blanket coverage.

The parametric insurance market (which uses similar trigger-based,
data-driven models) is projected to reach $22.6B in 2026, growing at 12.2%
CAGR to $63.8B by 2035.
[Source: Global Market Insights, Parametric Insurance Market, 2026]

This validates the market appetite for data-driven, trigger-based insurance
products and the reinsurance infrastructure to support them.

Conservative estimate: 15-20% reinsurance cost savings.

11. RISK FACTORS AND MITIGANTS

Risk: Regulatory approval for dynamic reallocation within one policy.
Mitigant: The product is structured as one composite general insurance policy.
Internal reallocation is portfolio management, not new policy issuance. ETFs
rebalance daily without per-trade regulatory approval. Same principle.

Risk: AI makes a wrong reallocation and customer is underprotected.
Mitigant: The floor-and-ceiling model guarantees minimum coverage in every
category. The AI enhances, it never strips. Worst case = the customer has
the same coverage as a traditional static policy at the floor levels.

Risk: Customer data privacy concerns.
Mitigant: HSBC already processes this transaction data for banking. No new
data collection is required. The AI reads merchant category codes and
amounts — the same data that already appears on the customer's statement.

Risk: Technology execution complexity.
Mitigant: Card authorization data arrives in 1-3 seconds. HSBC already
processes this in real time for fraud detection. The Fluid uses the same
data pipeline. HSBC's existing 5-minute claims processing in Hong Kong
proves the bank can execute real-time insurance operations.

Risk: Customers do not understand or trust the product.
Mitigant: The ETF analogy is powerful and widely understood. "Same money,
better protection" is a simple value proposition. Full transparency in the
app shows exactly where coverage is allocated at any moment.
