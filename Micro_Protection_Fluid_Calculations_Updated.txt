============================================================================

The coverage multiplier determines how much payout a customer receives per dollar
of allocated premium. These multipliers are benchmarked to existing HSBC products.

PART 2: PAYOUT FORMULA

============================================================================

----------------------------------------------------------------------
THE CORE FORMULA
----------------------------------------------------------------------

For Non-Parametric Categories (Health, Travel, Liability, Cyber, Property):

    Actual Payout = MIN(Actual Financial Loss, Allocated Coverage Amount × Multiplier)

For Parametric Categories (weather-triggered events within Travel or Property):

    Actual Payout = Allocated Coverage Amount × Multiplier × Trigger Percentage

KEY PRINCIPLE: The allocated coverage amount creates a MAXIMUM PAYOUT CEILING,
not a guaranteed payout. This is how real insurance works - you cannot claim
more than your policy limit.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
PAYOUT CEILINGS BY CATEGORY
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Category     | Minimum Payout ($5 allocation) | Maximum Payout ($90 allocation)
-------------|--------------------------------|--------------------------------
Life         | $2,500                         | $45,000
Liability    | $5,000                         | $90,000
Health       | $1,000                         | $18,000
Cyber        | $1,000                         | $18,000
Travel       | $750                           | $13,500
Property     | $500                           | $9,000

NOTE ON LIFE: Life insurance pays a lump sum death benefit. The $45,000
maximum is intentionally conservative - it supplements rather than replaces
HSBC's core life products.

PART 4: LIQUIDITY BUFFER MANAGEMENT
============================================================================

To support sub-second payouts, especially parametric triggers, the $110
short-term pool is maintained in a tiered liquidity structure.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
LIQUIDITY TIERS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Tier         | Size  | % of Pool | Asset Class           | Settlement | Use Case
-------------|-------|-----------|-----------------------|------------|------------------
Tier 1       | $22   | 20%       | Cash/Overnight deps   | Seconds    | Parametric payouts
(Instant)    |       |           |                       |            |
Tier 2       | $55   | 50%       | T-bills/Money market  | Hours      | Travel, accident,
(Same-day)   |       |           | funds                 |            | cyber claims
Tier 3       | $33   | 30%       | Ultra-short bonds     | 1-2 days   | Replenishment buffer
(Reserve)    |       |           |                       |            |

----------------------------------------------------------------------
REPLENISHMENT RULES
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Rule                    | Trigger                  | Action
------------------------|--------------------------|--------------------------------
Tier 1 replenishment    | Balance falls below $10  | Next premium replenishes to $22
Tier 2 replenishment    | Balance falls below $30  | Auto-transfer from Tier 3 (2AM)
Catastrophe override    | Multiple claims exhaust  | HSBC group liquidity (48hr access
                        | Tiers 1-2                |

----------------------------------------------------------------------
WHY THIS WORKS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Requirement                    | How Liquidity Buffer Addresses It
-------------------------------|------------------------------------------
Sub-second parametric payouts  | Tier 1 cash ($22) covers 10+ concurrent
                               | $2,200 parametric claims
No fire sale of long-term      | Short-term pool already liquid, no
assets                         | bond liquidation needed
Regulatory capital efficiency  | T-bills and MMF count as HQLA under
                               | HKIA rules
Customer trust                 | Claims paid instantly, improving app
                               | experience and retention

CONCLUSION
============================================================================

Micro Protection Fluid's SAA and Liquidity Management framework eliminates
ALM risk through structural design, not complex hedging. The $90 life pool
remains permanently invested in duration-matched bonds, untouched by
short-term claims.

The $110 shield pool sits in tiered liquid assets (20% instant cash, 50%
T-bills, 30% ultra-short bonds), capable of sub-second parametric payouts.

Every dollar of coverage is backed by a transparent multiplier benchmarked
to existing HSBC products. Every payout follows a clear formula:
MIN(actual loss, allocation × multiplier). Every category operates within
$5-$90 floors and ceilings, enforced by agentic AI within guardrails.

The result: a product that delivers real-time, dynamic insurance coverage
without compromising the foundational stability of long-term life funds.
